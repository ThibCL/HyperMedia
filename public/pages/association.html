Agir pour l'environnement

Presentation

Agir pour l'Environnement is a national citizens' association for environmental protection. The aim of the association is to put pressure on political leaders and economic decision-makers by carrying out citizen mobilisation campaigns bringing together the widest possible network of associations and citizens. Founded in February 1997 by some fifteen association leaders, the launch of Agir pour l'Environnement was made public by a sponsorship committee chaired by René Dumont and including Théodore Monod, Albert Jacquard, Edgar Morin and Pierre Rabhi. Agir pour l'Environnement has opted for total financial autonomy and today, more than 20,000 donors contribute to its independence, which allows us to preserve total structural and organizational autonomy from the powers that be.

History

The campaigns take the form of an educational document accompanied by competition cards that a network of around 40,000 signatories receives and sends back to us, to be sent to the campaign targets. The association's permanent team carries out, in parallel to this citizen mobilization, a pressure on decision-makers in order to obtain legislative and regulatory changes. To strengthen the balance of power, press conferences, actions, events, open letters, symposia, press kits and meetings are organised.
